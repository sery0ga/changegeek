Last Thursday I was approached by two guys at local startup gathering to discuss what a value of <a title="Kudo Box" href="http://www.management30.com/workout/kudo-box/" target="_blank">Kudobox</a> as a practice and a value of <a title="Kudobox" href="http://kudobox.co" target="_blank">online Kudobox</a> particularly is. There were a lot to say but one of the best stories I could tell them appeared the next day. And it was <a title="ACA IT Solutions" href="http://www.aca-it.be/" target="_blank">ACA IT-Solutions</a> which related it...

<p><a title="Employee Appreciation Day" href="http://hr.gwu.edu/employee-appreciation-day" target="_blank">Appreciation Day</a> is a nice practice of devoting a whole day to saying and sending appreciations and thank you messages to your colleagues. According to Jurgen Appelo, <a title="Kudo Box" href="http://www.management30.com/workout/kudo-box/" target="_blank">Kudo Cards</a> are a great tool to use that time because <strong>kudos are small public rewards provided by peers</strong> i.e. people who know much better how you do your job than your managers.</p>
<p><a title="Kudobox" href="http://kudobox.co" target="_blank">Kudobox</a> brings physical kudo cards to a new level, allowing to share your appreciation instantly on Twitter and let the whole world know how cool your colleagues are.</p>
<p>What did smart guys from <a title="ACA IT-Solutions" href="http://www.aca-it.be/" target="_blank">ACA IT-Solutions</a> do?</p>
<p>They used <a title="Kudobox" href="http://kudobox.co" target="_blank">Kudobox</a> to send appreciations to each other through Twitter saying 'You rock! Thanks a lot for helping me with...'</p>

<p>The kudos started multiple discussions and helped many employees at <a title="ACA IT-Solutions" href="http://www.aca-it.be/" target="_blank">ACA IT-Solutions</a> to recall funny or challenging moments of their collaboration. <strong><br />

<p>I don't know how they feel but for me getting a kudo is like remembering a good story while drinking beer in a cozy pub. Image it and whoala... <strong>an appreciation day becomes an awesome beer party full of jokes and good stories</strong>.</p>
<p>What a great time! What a nice idea!</p>
<h2>Should you wait for another #appreciation day?</h2>
<p>Of course not!</p>
<p>The more you wait for saying 'thank you', the more chances your colleagues will help you with something else and you'll get another reason to be thankful for.</p>
<p>Do not wait for a year, a month or even a day.</p>
